Section 1: History and Chronology
Q1: The Crash and the Revival

The 1983 Video Game Crash was caused by market oversaturation, poor-quality games, and loss of consumer trust. The industry was revived by Nintendo with the NES, introducing Mario as its mascot.

Q2: Era Differentiation

Golden Age of Arcades: Pac-Man — played in public arcades

Modern 3D & Online Era: World of Warcraft — played in private spaces online
Difference: public, physical social play vs. private, internet-based social play.

Q3: The 16-bit Wars

Companies/Consoles: Nintendo (SNES) and Sega (Genesis)

PC games: Doom (FPS) and Civilization (Strategy)

Section 2: Genre Classification & Evolution
Q4: Genre Definitions

Metroidvania: Exploration with ability-based progression — Super Metroid

JRPG: Story-driven RPG with turn-based combat — Final Fantasy VII

RTS: Real-time unit and resource control — StarCraft

Q5: Roguelike vs Roguelite

Roguelikes reset progress on death. Roguelites keep permanent upgrades between runs (Hades).

Q6: Souls-like

Defining game: Demon’s Souls

Core elements: High-difficulty combat and interconnected world with death penalties

Section 3: Influence and Key Titles
Q7: 3D & Open Worlds

3D graphics enabled large explorable worlds. Key title: GTA III.

Q8: Modding & New Genres

The MOBA genre came from Warcraft III mods (DotA).

Q9: Pillars of Gaming

Tetris: Skill-based, universal design

Ocarina of Time: 3D world design and storytelling
